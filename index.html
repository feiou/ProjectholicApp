Frequently asked questions

Quick answers about Projectholic, who it's for, and how it works.

What is Projectholic?

Projectholic turns the projects in your head into a clear plan you can actually finish. Set a goal, break it into milestones, and schedule the work against the real hours you have this week, not the optimistic version. It runs natively on iPhone, iPad, and Mac with iCloud sync, and it's free to download on the App Store.

Is Projectholic free?

Yes, fully free on the App Store. No trial timer, no upsell wall, no premium tier waiting to interrupt you. Planning, milestones, scheduling, gantt-style timeline, progress tracking, iCloud sync, all included. Built by an independent studio that earns its keep by being genuinely useful, not by adding friction to make you pay.

Which Apple devices does Projectholic support?

iPhone, iPad, and Mac, with iCloud sync between all three. Plan on the train, adjust on the couch, ship at the desk. Your projects move with you, no setup required. Each platform uses native conventions, so it feels right whether you're on a Mac trackpad or an iPad with Apple Pencil. Try it on whichever you have nearest first.

Does Projectholic support gantt charts?

Yes, but the kind that helps you finish, not the kind you spend three days maintaining. Place milestones, drop tasks under them, and see the timeline against your real available hours. It's gantt-style thinking without enterprise overhead. Most people have their first project mapped within ten minutes of opening the app.

Can Projectholic sync with Apple Calendar?

Yes, two-way sync. Tasks you schedule in Projectholic appear in Apple Calendar alongside meetings and personal events, and changes flow back into the plan. That's how schedules stay honest. Your project hours meet the rest of your week, and you stop overcommitting Wednesdays. Setup takes about thirty seconds the first time you open the app.

Who makes Projectholic?

Feioustudio is an independent practice founded by Feiou Su, with a background in interaction design, AR/VR, and product design. The studio also makes Watermelon Todo, NearbyGem, and Udoland, but Projectholic is the one built for people who want to actually finish what they start. Read the story on the about page, then download the app and try it.
